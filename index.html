History of the First Website

The first World Wide Web was invented in 1989 and introduced to the world on August 6, 1991 at the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN). It was created by the British computer scientist Tim Berners-Lee on his Next computer designed by Steve Jobs. The web was originally developed for universities and institutes in all the world as a solution for a demand for automated information-sharing between scientists. In 1992 there were only 10 websites. In 1993, CERN put the WWW software in the public domain and later on made a release available with an open license. By 1996 there were already 2 million websites.
